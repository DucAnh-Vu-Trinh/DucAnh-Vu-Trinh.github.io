PIFA Desgin for Dual-Band Operation in Mobile 2024 Mar - 2024 May

  • Objective: The aim is to design a PIFA capable of operating at two desired frequencies: 0.9 GHz and 1.8 GHz, corresponding to LTE bands 3 and 8 respectively.
  • Antenna Structure: The metal plate is situated at the bottom of the substrate with a width of 7 mm. A shorting pin or plate is employed to short the planar antenna to the ground, while a feeding pin provides energy for the antenna via coaxial cable.
  • Simulation and Optimization: Theoretical foundations and experimental optimization techniques are utilized, considering various design parameters such as substrate material, feeding mechanism, shorting configuration, and slot design.
  • Performance Evaluation: Simulation results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed designs in achieving dual-band functionality, considering return loss, radiation efficiency, and bandwidth characteristics.
  • Challenges and Solutions: Challenges such as narrow bandwidth and low radiation efficiency are identified, with efforts made to optimize the design through the addition of parasitic elements and slot modifications.
